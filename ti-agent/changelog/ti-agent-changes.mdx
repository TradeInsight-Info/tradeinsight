#### v0.2.2 — 2026-07-22

- Stock-analysis reports now open in your browser the moment they're generated — no hunting for the saved file (and if your system can't open it, ti tells you the exact path instead).
- Your **AI plan now unlocks paid-grade market data** — an active AI Pro or Enterprise plan (or an unexpired trial) gives you the same deep price and filing history as a paid data plan.
- Higher ticker limits — free and beta users can now query up to 300 tickers.
- Clearer limit messages — hit your monthly ticker limit and ti explains it and points you to an upgrade; brief rate limits simply tell you to retry.

#### v0.2.1 — 2026-07-21

- New **/watch** command — keep a watchlist that syncs across your sessions: `/watch AAPL` to add, `/watch` to see it, `/watch remove AAPL` to drop one. It only accepts real ticker symbols, so a stray phrase won't clutter your list.
- New **/help** command — a quick signpost for getting started or when you're unsure what to ask: what ti is, the core commands, and example prompts to copy.
- New **technical-analysis** skill — ti computes indicators (moving averages, RSI, MACD, Bollinger Bands, and more) and can build and backtest signals from them.
- Options pricing now covers **American-style options** (early exercise, via a binomial model) alongside European Black-Scholes — both with the Greeks. The skill is now simply **options**.
- More trustworthy backtests — an additional engine suited to longer-horizon strategies, more realistic execution and cost modeling, and graceful fallback when an optional dependency is missing, so results track reality more closely.
- Faster price-history fetches — ti fills only the missing date ranges from its local cache instead of re-downloading, so repeat requests return quickly.

#### v0.2.0 — 2026-07-18

- New **/capabilities** command — see everything ti can do at a glance: its tools, loaded skills, and finance-knowledge concepts, listed live from what's actually available in your session so it's never out of date.
- New **finance_knowledge** tool — ti grounds its answers in curated TradeInsight finance concepts (market strategies and patterns, hedging, derivatives, bonds) instead of relying on the model's memory alone.
- Your TradeInsight sign-in now refreshes automatically in the background, so you stay logged in across sessions without re-authenticating.
- On an authentication failure, finance tools now stop cleanly and tell you — instead of silently falling back to less reliable web data — so an answer is never built on the wrong source.

#### v0.1.6 — 2026-07-16

- Polished the npm package page — clearer README with a quick-start, plus homepage and docs links.

#### v0.1.5 — 2026-07-16

- `ti` now installs from the public npm registry — no token or registry setup required. Just run `npm install -g @tradeinsight-info/ti` (or `bun install -g @tradeinsight-info/ti`).

#### v0.1.3 — 2026-07-16

- New **market-cycles** skill — ask about calendar and seasonal patterns ("sell in May" / the Halloween indicator, the January effect, the BTC halving cycle, the presidential-election cycle) and ti backtests the claim on real data with proper scrutiny for tiny samples and data-mining, instead of repeating the folklore.
- Value-investing analysis now engages on more natural phrasings — moat, intrinsic / fair value, margin of safety, or "is X a good long-term buy".

#### v0.1.0 — 2026-07-15

- Meet **ti** — a finance-first analysis and quant agent that researches and backtests strategies, builds and stress-tests portfolios, and makes sense of market data. It writes code in service of insight, and never places orders or connects to a broker — you stay in control.
- Backtest a strategy or a full portfolio allocation on historical data — risk-parity, min-vol, vol-target, momentum, DCA/AIP, and leverage — in one local framework.
- Verify market strategies, cycles, and seasonal claims (e.g. sell-in-May) by backtesting them on real data instead of taking the folklore at face value.
- Size and backtest hedges — market-beta, diversifiers, exposure trims — to cut downside risk.
- Price and reason about options locally: Black-Scholes value, the Greeks, implied volatility, and multi-leg payoffs (covered calls, spreads, straddles).
- Reason about fixed income — bonds, the price↔yield relationship, duration and rate risk — and pick a bond ETF for a goal.
- Analyze companies and long-term holdings through a value-investing (Buffett/Munger) lens.
- **/profile** tailors the agent to how you actually invest (archetype, risk, return target, frequency, horizon); **/login-ti** and **/logout-all** manage your TradeInsight sign-in and connected LLM provider.
- Fetch daily OHLCV price history (cached locally), multi-symbol quotes and detailed single-symbol snapshots (50/100/200-day averages, 52-week high/low), and recent congressional & corporate-insider trades by ticker — quotes are exchange-delayed (~15 min for US).
- Bring your own LLM provider (BYOK) and sign in to TradeInsight to sync your profile across sessions.
